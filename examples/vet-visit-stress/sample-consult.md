# Sample Consult: Vet Visit Stress

This is a fictional, shortened example. It shows the expected shape of the
answer; it is not veterinary advice for a real animal.

## Bottom Line

This case needs a low-stress handling plan before the next routine visit, and a
veterinarian should decide whether pre-visit medication is appropriate. Panting
and urination in the carrier suggest high distress, so the plan should reduce
predictable triggers rather than relying on restraint at the clinic.

## Plan

- Leave the carrier out permanently as furniture; add bedding and treats inside.
- Feed high-value treats near the carrier, then inside it, then with the door
  briefly closed.
- Practice short, non-veterinary car steps: carrier in car, engine off; engine
  on; short drive; return home.
- Ask the clinic for cat-friendly scheduling: quiet waiting, covered carrier,
  minimal dog exposure, gentle handling, and stopping if distress escalates.
- Discuss pre-visit medication with the veterinarian if training alone is not
  enough or the visit is urgent.
- Avoid flooding, forced extraction, punishment, or repeated long drives while
  the cat is panicking.
